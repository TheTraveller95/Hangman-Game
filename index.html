The Hangman Game

Game rules

The rule of this old but funny game are very simple:
Depending on the chosen level, the computer chooses from a list of words about different topics a random one. The player has to guess what the word is one letter at a time. A number of dashes equivalent to the number of letters in the word will be displayed. If the guessing player chooses a letter that occurs in the word, the computer will replace the dash in the right places on the word. If the word does not contain the suggested letter, one element of a hangmanâ€™s gallows will be displayed. As the game progresses, a segment of the victim is added for every suggested letter not in the word. The number of incorrect guesses before the game ends is 7.

Game story

In 17th and 18th century Europe, criminals who were sentenced to death by hanging could demand the "Rite of Words and Life." In the "Rite of Words and Life" a criminal to be hanged was strung up over a 5 legged stand, and a board with a series of short ropes representing a word was displayed. The Executioner would pick the word, and would use charcoal to mark correctly guessed letters on the dashes of the board, and incorrect letters to the side. At every incorrect guess the executioner would use a sledge hammer or axe to knock away a single leg of the stand. 5 wrong guesses, and the criminal was hanged. If the entire word was filled in correctly or guessed, the condemned would be set free from that sentence and not tried again on that crime. The irony here was that almost all of those criminals were illiterate. Either way, we have preserved that old tradition in our own small way.
